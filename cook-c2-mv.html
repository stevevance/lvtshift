Cook County: per-taxing-district land value tax — market-value base (C2-MV)

Modeled impact of each of Cook County's ~900 taxing bodies (school, library, park, municipal, county districts) shifting its own levy to land within its own boundary — revenue-neutral per district. The reform is applied per taxing body; the results below are merely summarized by assessment township (34 of them) for navigation — the township is not the tax unit. Unlike the single county-wide rate (C1), this preserves every district's revenue and the link between a parcel and the districts that tax it, so it isolates the pure land shift rather than also pooling tax base across the county. A parcel's new bill = the sum, over the districts covering it, of its land value × each district's land rate. Two reforms: a 2.5:1 split-rate (land taxed 2.5× buildings, the Illinois classification cap) and a 100% building abatement (pure land tax). Positive = pays more.

How to read this. A land shift taxes land at a higher rate than buildings, set so total revenue is unchanged. Because the total is unchanged, it's a redistribution: whoever is land-heavy pays more, whoever is building-heavy pays less.
  • A vacant lot is 100% land → taxed entirely at the high land rate → pays more.
  • A high-rise condo is ~90% building → most of its value is taxed at the low building rate → pays less.
  • A typical single-family home (~25% land) → modestly less.
